About the Founder

Who I Am

I am Shyam Sundar R, Founder of ScholarVault — an academic technology initiative focused on creating a trustworthy and modern ecosystem for researchers, faculty members, students, institutions, and conference communities.

My professional journey began in customer support and conference operations, where I gained hands-on experience working with research conferences, academic communities, and institutional stakeholders. These experiences revealed significant challenges within the research ecosystem — including fragmented resources, predatory conferences, limited visibility for scholars, and disconnected academic networks.

To address these challenges, I founded ScholarVault on 26 February 2026 with a vision of building a trusted digital infrastructure that empowers academic growth, research collaboration, scholarly visibility, and conference transparency.

Today, ScholarVault is actively serving researchers while expanding its platform, partnerships, and academic initiatives across India and beyond.

Strategic Narrative

Why I Started ScholarVault

During my years in customer support and academic conference operations, I had a front-row seat to the daily friction points that researchers, professors, and student scholars face.

I saw brilliant minds lose months of work to predatory publishers, fail to discover genuine peer-reviewed conferences, and navigate a highly fragmented academic ecosystem. Trust was incredibly hard to verify, and access to key opportunities was unequal.

ScholarVault was founded to build a transparent, researcher-first ecosystem. We are building the modern trust infrastructure and digital workflows that enable researchers to collaborate safely, build verified credibility, and push scientific boundaries forward.

Landscape Challenge

The Research Integrity Challenge

Why Trust Matters More Than Ever

The global academic ecosystem is expanding rapidly, creating unprecedented opportunities for researchers, institutions, and conference organizers. At the same time, researchers face growing challenges in identifying trustworthy conferences, journals, and academic opportunities.

Many scholars encounter:

  • warning Unverified conference invitations & misleading publication claims
  • warning Fragmented academic resources scattered across disconnected systems
  • warning Limited visibility into conference quality, indexing, and peer-review credibility
  • warning Difficulty discovering relevant opportunities across diverse academic disciplines

As research participation continues to grow worldwide, transparency, verification, and trust have become essential components of the modern academic landscape. ScholarVault was created to address these challenges through verification frameworks, conference intelligence, academic discovery tools, and researcher-focused trust infrastructure.
